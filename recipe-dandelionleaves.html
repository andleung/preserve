15 min(Serves 4)


Ingredients

Large bunch of fresh dandelion leaves
1/2 head of garlic, roughly chopped
2 tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil
Red pepper flakes
Salt

01.

Dandelion leaves make for a unique side dish. Try foraging for them yourself, but make sure to avoid areas that have been treated with pesticide and wash throughly before use.

The leaves have a bitter taste that may not be for everyone, but they are a wonderful source of vitamins and minerals and powerful anti-inflammatory. They have a rich, complex flavor for those that embrace the bitterness.

Wash the leaves and roughly chop into 2-3 inch pieces.

In a large saucepan over medium heat, simmer the leaves with a small amount of water until tender and water has evaporated, about 10 minutes.

02.

Add garlic, red pepper flakes, a pinch of salt, and a swirl of olive oil to the pan. Stirring, cook until garlic is golden brown, about 5 minutes.

03.

Let cool and serve. Dandelion leaves are a wonderful compliment to fatty meats like bacon, and thick sauces like marinara.
