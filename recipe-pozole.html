2.5 hr(Serves 4)


Ingredients

6 dried California chiles
3 lb. pork shoulder
3 (15oz.) cans hominy, drained
1 large yellow onion, quartered
1 head of garlic, roughly chopped
4 cups chicken broth
1 tsp. salt
1 tsp. cumin
1 tsp. smoked paprika
1 tbsp. oregano
3 bay leaves
1 can of beer

Cabbage, radish, onion,
cilantro, & lime to garnish.

01.

Try this traditional Latin soup with rich, mildly spicy broth, tender pork shoulder, and all the fixings of your favorite tacos.

Heat a dry pan and lightly toast the peppers. Do not char; it will make them bitter. Remove from pan and let cool. Cut in half length-wise and remove stems and seeds.

Place the peppers in a small pot covered with water and bring to a simmer. Remove from heat and let peppers soak. When cooled, transfer the peppers and soaking liquid to a blender and blend until smooth.

02.

Season pork shoulder with salt, paprika, and cumin. In a large pot heat some oil and sear the pork on all sides. Add onion and garlic, stir, and cook an additional 2 to 3 minutes. Add half the can of beer to the pot and drink the other half.

When beer has reduced, add the blended peppers, canned hominy, chicken broth, oregano, and a few bay leaves. Add a few cups of water as needed to cover pork. Simmer on low heat for about 2 hours.

03.

Remove pork from the pot and shred into large chunks with a fork. Return to the pot and simmer for another 30 minutes.

Garnish with thinly sliced cabbage and radishes, diced onion, cilantro, and a lime wedge.
